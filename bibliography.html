Apologetics Bibliography

The first assertion is where he notes that all Christian believers are, in fact, atheists, as it pertains to any number of historically known deities.

Get Started List...

  • The Scriptures - Read them all, Genesis to Revelation
  • Lee Stroebel:
  • The Case for Christ
  • The Case for Faith
  • The Case for a Creator
  • The Case for the Real Jesus
  • The Case for Miracles
  • NOTE: You should take the time to read all of these. Each book answers different questions. To know where to find the answer to a question being raised (in some future class or conversation) will be a product of having familiarized yourself with the ground Strobel covers. He's given us a wonderful gift. It's a basic Christian apologetics anthology. He interviews many of the leading Christian apologists and raises the salient questions you'll encounter.
  • C.S. Lewis:
  • Mere Christianity
  • God in the Dock
  • Note: Read as much of C. S. Lewis as you can get your hands on.
  • Assorted Authors:
  • I Don't Have Enough FAITH to be an ATHEIST, by Norman Geisler and Frank Turek
  • The REASON for GOD, by Timothy Keller
  • On Guard, William Lane Craig
  • Reason to Believe by R. C. Sproul:
  • Handbook of Christian Apologetics by Peter Kreeft & Ronald Tacelli
  • Jesus's Resurrection by Copan & Tacelli
  • Seeking Allah, Finding Jesus, Nabeel Qureshi
  • Do You Still Think God Is Good? by Clayton Brumby
  • GOD: The Evidence, by Patrick Glynn
  • Answers for Atheists, Agnostics and Other Thoughtful Skeptics by Calvin Beisner (out of print)
  • Know Why You Believe by Paul Little

Science and Darwinism...

  • Icons of Evolution
  • The Politically Incorrect Guide to Darwinism and Intelligent Design
  • The Myth of Junk DNA, by Jonathan Wells
  • Evolution: A Theory in Crisis by Michael Denton
  • Note: Excellent read and very even handed. Both Phillip Johnson and Michael Behe credit Michael Denton with getting them thinking. One of my favorites. Michael Denton is an agnostic.
  • Darwin's Black Box, by Michael Behe
  • The Edge of Evolution, by Michael Behe
  • Darwin Devolves, by Michael Behe
  • Note: Michael Behe initiated the idea of Irreducible Complexity. He was featured in Strobel's, The Case for a Creator, is a Roman Catholic, and believes in common descent - that humans and apes shared a common ancestor.
  • Darwin on Trial, by Phillip Johnson
  • Defeating Darwinism by Opening Minds, by Phillip Johnson
  • Note: Phillip Johnson is a Christian, taught at UC Berkeley, and was the first non-scientist to publically take on the Darwinian establishment. Darwin on Trial is a must read.
  • Undeniable, Doug Axe
  • God's Undertaker, Has Science Buried God? John Lennox
  • Gunning for God by John Lennox
  • God and Stephen Hawking, John Lennox
  • Signature in the Cell
  • Darwin's Doubt, Stephen Meyer
  • The Mystery of Life's Origin, Thaxton, Bradley and Olsen
  • Not by Chance, Lee Spetner
  • God and the Astronomers, Robert Jastrow
